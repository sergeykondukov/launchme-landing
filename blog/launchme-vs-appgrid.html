Where AppGrid falls short in daily usage

Many practical controls are in AppGrid Pro

According to AppGrid's own feature list, multi-select drag and drop, grouping tools, remove-from-grid actions, and full-grid save/export options are Pro-focused. This creates a split workflow where core cleanup and large-library management can become upgrade-gated.

Activation and gesture flow are less integrated

AppGrid's FAQ states that pinch gesture and hot-corner style trigger currently need a third-party app. LaunchMe provides built-in Hot Corners and Gesture Invoke in the Free tier, which makes open-and-launch flow faster and simpler.

Edit-mode heavy interaction

AppGrid follows a traditional edit-mode approach for many organization actions. This works, but can slow down frequent tweaks when your app library grows. LaunchMe is optimized for quicker day-to-day iteration with easier visual tuning and workflow-oriented settings.

Why LaunchMe is better for modern macOS workflow

  • Built-in utilities in Free: Search Files, Clipboard, and Calculator reduce context switching.
  • Activation speed in Free: Hot Corners and Gesture Invoke are available without extra tools.
  • Customization depth: widgets, wallpapers, icon styling, and visual themes scale better for power users.
  • Layout control: categories, layout options, and clean visual organization for large app collections.

Multi-language support

LaunchMe supports 11 languages: English, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, German, Italian, Spanish, Russian, Portuguese, Hindi, and Turkish. This helps teams and individual users keep a familiar interface across different locales.
